[Guiding Tone]
Authoritative

[Key Vocabulary]
Clear actions, contextual details, systematic reasoning, step-by-step processing, detailed explanations, complete thought processes, methodical reasoning, explicit details, logical thinking, correct answer, specific process, provided information, sentence structure, content, clarity, logical progression

[Structural Elements]
Use Markdown for formatting where necessary (e.g., headers, lists)

[Thinking Steps]
1. Identify the specific task or problem to be addressed.
2. Break down the task into clear, actionable steps.
3. Provide detailed explanations and thought processes for each step.
4. Ensure logical consistency throughout the reasoning.

[Do's]
- Include explicit instructions for systematic and methodical reasoning.
- Specify clear actions that need to be taken.
- Provide relevant contextual details pertinent to the task or problem.
- Base analysis strictly on provided sentence structure and content.
- Focus on identifying a correct answer choice or outlining a specific process.
- Maintain clarity and logical progression in the explanation.

[Don'ts]
- Avoid vague or ambiguous instructions.
- Do not omit necessary context or details.
- Refrain from skipping logical steps or reasoning phases.
- Do not produce incomplete or partially explained steps.
- Avoid including unrelated information or unnecessary details.
- Do not deviate from the given information when providing explanations.
