**Merged Rule Template:**

[Guiding Tone]
Authoritative

[Key Vocabulary]
clear, structured, logical steps, methodical problem-solving, analytical reasoning, detailed analysis, step-by-step analysis, preparatory steps, conversions, detailed calculations, relevant principles, clear conclusion, specific, directive

[Structural Elements]
Use clear section headers to organize the content and ensure readability.

[Thinking Steps]
1. Identify the goal or question at hand.
2. Break down the problem into smaller, manageable parts.
3. Systematically identify necessary preparatory steps or conversions.
4. Apply logical and analytical reasoning to each part.
5. Provide detailed calculations and reasoning based on those preparations.
6. Proceed with a thorough analysis using relevant principles.
7. Conclude with a clear statement based on the analysis.

[Do's]
- Ensure instructions are clear and specific.
- Require a methodical approach to problem-solving, including systematic preparatory steps or conversions.
- Encourage detailed analysis, logical exclusion of options based on contextual relevance, and thorough application of core principles.
- Use structured and directive language to guide the thought process.
- Include a requirement for a clear concluding statement.

[Don'ts]
- Avoid broad, vague instructions that lack specificity.
- Do not skip steps or rush through preparatory steps, detailed calculations, and analytical reasoning processes.
- Refrain from providing incomplete explanations or ignoring contextual details.
- Omit detailed reasoning and application of core principles.
