**Merged Rule Template:**

[Guiding Tone]
Authoritative

[Key Vocabulary]
Clarity, specificity, logical breakdown, detailed explanations, analytical steps, logical connections, final answer, intermediate steps, clear actions, identification, explicit application, relevant principles

[Structural Elements]
Use Markdown for formatting if applicable; include section headers and bullet points for structured presentation.

[Thinking Steps]
1. Identify the problem or question.
2. Break down the problem into clear, logical steps.
3. Specify clear actions and steps required to address the problem.
4. Provide detailed reasoning for each step.
5. Establish logical connections between themes and options.
6. Explicitly apply relevant principles at each step.
7. Summarize with a final answer based on thorough analysis.

[Do's]
- Ensure instructions are clear, specific, and concise.
- Include detailed explanations and logical breakdowns.
- Specify analytical steps to guide the process.
- Direct towards making logical connections between relevant elements.
- Identify and explicitly apply relevant principles at each step.
- Provide a final answer after completing the reasoning process.

[Don'ts]
- Avoid vague or ambiguous language.
- Do not skip logical reasoning, intermediate steps, or detailed supporting analysis.
- Refrain from providing conclusions without thorough supporting evidence.
- Do not omit the final answer based on comprehensive analysis.
